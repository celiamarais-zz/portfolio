IfOnly Discovery Flow

IfOnly is an online experience marketplace, and as such, its inventory is highly variable: it relies on vendors’ availability and location. Our customers weren't able to reliably find experiences that were accessible to them.
A brainstorm session and a little bit of research confirmed our main perceived pain points, and uncovered new ones. We built high-fidelity prototypes on which we iterated as we tested them with users.
We created a completely new, scalable and flexible discovery experience, unified across all screen sizes. We also refreshed the visual design of the site, and redefined IfOnly’s online branding. I also oversaw the implementation of the new experience.
We introduced a number of new navigation and search tools.
A rotating tagline on our marquee contextualizes IfOnly's different types of experiences, so visitors can see right away how and why they would enjoy one.
A prominent discovery module on our home page suggests categories, shops, or occasions, and seamlessly filters out unavailable experiences before users start browsing
I established an online styleguide and laid out the rules for a modular layout and an adaptive grid system. Together with the engineering team, we rationalized IfOnly’s CSS structure.
“Hey, there are things for me in there!” In user testing, the response was overwhelmingly positive. Users consistently perceived IfOnly as much bigger, and more accessible than before.
